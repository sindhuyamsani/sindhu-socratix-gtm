Distribution Leverage · Inbound Without Cold Outreach

Three warm partnerships hiding in the integration list.

Socratix already integrates with 20 platforms. Three of them have massive distribution, no investigation layer, and an incentive to co-sell. These aren't cold pitches — they're relationships that already exist.

Stripe
Infrastructure · Already integrated · Massive distribution
Stripe Radar detects payment fraud. Stripe has zero investigation layer. Every Stripe customer hitting chargeback thresholds or account review backlogs is a warm Socratix prospect — and they already trust Stripe. An App Marketplace listing puts Socratix in front of that distribution without a single cold email.
What Stripe gets
A complete fraud story for enterprise customers: detection (Radar) + investigation (Socratix). No build required. Closes a gap their largest customers feel.
Alloy
Compliance Automation · Already integrated · Exact ICP overlap
Alloy automates KYC and onboarding. They stop at the front door. Socratix handles the fraud that walks in after onboarding. Every Alloy customer with a fraud ops team is a Socratix prospect — and Alloy's ICP (fintechs, neobanks, lenders) is an exact match. The co-sell motion is natural: "onboarding solved, ongoing ops next."
What Alloy gets
An upsell story and platform stickiness: "We handle onboarding. Our partner handles what happens after." Expands Alloy's value without competing with Socratix.
Socure
Identity Verification · Already integrated · Alert-to-resolution gap
Socure scores identity risk at onboarding. When they flag a medium-risk account, someone still has to investigate it manually. Socratix closes that gap. The handoff is natural: Socure surfaces the alert, Socratix works it. Co-selling makes both products stickier for shared customers.
What Socure gets
A resolution story for the alerts they generate. Customers stop asking "OK, you flagged it, now what?" — a complaint every identity vendor hears constantly.
Why start here: All three partners are already in Socratix's integration list, meaning technical trust is established. The ask isn't "will you integrate with us?" — it's "will you list us in your marketplace and mention us in your enterprise conversations?" That's a much lighter ask, and the mutual value is clear for each. Stripe is the highest-distribution play; Alloy is the fastest co-sell motion given ICP alignment; Socure is the most conceptually clean narrative.
